Pick an accessibility tier

Each tier is a hard commitment, not a wish. Whatever we pick becomes a CI gate — components that violate it don't ship. The button to the right of each label is shown at the actual minimum touch-target size for that tier, so you can feel the difference with your thumb on a phone if you open this URL on mobile.

Sacrifices visual density. AAA contrast restricts the palette — indigo on cream may need darkening, accents may need pruning. Best for elderly/low-vision-first products.
My recommendation: 2 (Inclusive). Tier 1 is too thin for the maid persona — 44px is the minimum a young, dexterous, sighted thumb can hit reliably; the gap to real-world conditions is large. Tier 3 is excellent but the AAA contrast requirement will fight our chosen palette (indigo on cream is ~5.8:1, which is AA but not AAA — we'd have to darken the indigo and lose some of the palette personality). Tier 2 keeps the palette intact, doubles real-world usability, and adds the rules that prevent the most common accessibility regressions (icon-only buttons without labels, color-only error states).
