Correction (post-brainstorm): the page below claims IBM Plex Sans covers Bengali script. That's wrong — IBM Plex covers Latin, Devanagari, Arabic, Cyrillic, Greek, Hebrew, Thai, CJK, but not Bengali. My recommendation: 1 (IBM Plex Sans). Two reasons. (1) Multilingual coherence: IBM Plex is one of the few open-source families designed by a single team across Latin, Bengali, Devanagari, etc. — they all share rhythm, weight, and proportions, so a maid switching to Manipuri sees the *same app*, not a fallback. (2) Trust-context fit: IBM Plex was literally designed to carry a trust/utility brand. Geist is gorgeous for Latin but the moment Bengali appears the system looks inconsistent. We'd replace Geist (currently `--font-sans`) with IBM Plex Sans. Mizo uses regular Latin with a few diacritics so any of the three handles it fine.

Note: Manipuri samples shown in Bengali script for browser-font availability. Meitei Mayek (the traditional script) can be added later as an in-locale toggle once we have Noto Sans Meetei Mayek bundled — captured as a future decision in the i18n spec (slice D).
